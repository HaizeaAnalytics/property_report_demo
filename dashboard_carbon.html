Early-stage funding may be available through carbon buyers willing to purchase future credits.

ERF Eligibility Assessment

‚úì Environmental Plantings - Likely Eligible
  • 85 hectares of cleared land identified
  • Land has not been forested in recent history (check satellite records)
  • Suitable for native tree establishment based on soil and climate
  • Method: "Plantation Forestry" or "Environmental Plantings" under ERF
  • Requires: Confirmation land was cleared before 1990 (or meets other eligibility criteria)
‚ö† Human Induced Regeneration - Assessment Required
  • 25 hectares of sparse woodland identified as potential HIR area
  • Method: "HIR" under ERF requires proof regeneration is "human-induced" (e.g., grazing exclusion)
  • Must demonstrate vegetation would not regenerate under business-as-usual management
  • More complex eligibility requirements than environmental plantings
  • Requires: Detailed assessment by ERF specialist to confirm eligibility
‚úó Existing Forest - Not Eligible
  • 116 hectares of existing forest already sequestering carbon
  • Not eligible under current ERF methods (no "additionality")
  • May be eligible for "Avoided Deforestation" if clearing is legally permitted but foregone (rare circumstances)
  • Could potentially be included in future biodiversity conservation schemes

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Notice - Not Financial or Investment Advice

This assessment provides biophysical carbon sequestration potential based on FullCAM modeling, land suitability analysis, and current carbon balance estimates. It does not constitute financial, investment, or project advice.

Carbon credit prices are volatile and future prices are uncertain.

Project returns depend on numerous factors including:

  • Establishment and ongoing costs (site-specific)
  • Carbon price at time of ACCU issuance
  • Project management fees and approach
  • Opportunity costs (foregone agricultural income)
  • Regulatory changes and policy settings
  • Actual sequestration rates (may differ from projections)
  • Risk of reversal (fire, drought, disease)

Always consult qualified professionals:

  • ERF-registered carbon project specialist for detailed feasibility assessment and eligibility confirmation
  • Financial advisor for investment analysis and financial projections
  • Legal advisor for contract review and permanence obligations
  • Tax advisor for tax implications of carbon income

Carbon projects involve long-term commitments (25-100 years) that bind current and future landowners. Carefully consider all implications before proceeding.
