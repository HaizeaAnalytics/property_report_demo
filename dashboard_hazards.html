Risk assessment: Overall moderate fire risk. 42% of property (forested areas) rated high to extreme risk. Risk highest in north-western woodland section. Improved pasture areas (52%) provide natural fire breaks. Asset protection zones recommended around structures in or near extreme risk areas. Property experienced one significant fire event in past 25 years (2019, 15% of property burnt).

Fire History (2000-2024)

December 2019
Major fire event during extreme fire season. 41.2 ha burnt (15% of property). Primarily woodland areas in north-west section. Est. biomass loss: 185 tonnes. No structures affected. Regrowth evident in subsequent years.
January 2013
Minor fire in south-eastern boundary (3.8 ha, 1.4% of property). Controlled burn that escaped containment. Low intensity, minimal damage.
2003, 2006, 2009, 2017
Fires occurred within 5km of property boundary but did not reach property. Regional fire seasons with elevated risk but no direct impact.
Regional fire context: Mudgee LGA experiences bushfire activity approximately every 3-4 years. Property has been directly affected twice in 25 years (2013, 2019). Most recent significant event was 2019 Black Summer fires. Fire season typically November-March, peak risk in December-January during hot, dry, windy conditions.

Fuel Moisture & Flammability Patterns

Flammability trends: Fuel moisture content (FMC) averages 92% in wet season (Jun-Sep), drops to 45% in fire season (Dec-Feb). Critically low FMC (<50%) occurs average 42 days per year, predominantly December-February. 2019 event occurred after 18 consecutive days of FMC <40% combined with extreme temperatures and high winds. Current year FMC within normal range for season.

Overall Hazard Profile & Recommendations

üî• Bushfire - Moderate Risk:
  • 42% of property high to extreme risk (forested areas)
  • One major fire event in 25 years (2019)
  • Fire season December-February, peak risk during heatwaves
  • Recommendations: Maintain asset protection zones around buildings, fuel reduction in high-risk areas, ember-resistant construction materials, bushfire survival plan
üíß Flood - Low Risk:
  • 88% of property never flooded in 25-year record
  • High-frequency areas limited to 2% (creek flats)
  • Recommendations: Avoid development in identified flood-prone areas, maintain drainage, consider dam spillway adequacy for extreme rainfall events
üå°Ô∏è Extreme Heat - Increasing Trend:
  • Hot days increased from 72 to 89 per year over 25 years
  • Heatwaves becoming more frequent (4+ per year)
  • Implications: Livestock heat stress, increased water demand, elevated fire risk, infrastructure heat loading
  • Recommendations: Provide shade for stock, ensure adequate water supply, heat-resilient pasture species
üí® Wind & Storm - Moderate Risk:
  • Extreme gusts (>120 km/h) occur occasionally (~1 per 5-10 years)
  • Tree fall hazard in forested areas during storms
  • Recommendations: Remove dead/hazardous trees near structures, secure loose materials, design buildings for N2 wind region (AS/NZS 1170.2)
üí° Insurance & Planning Considerations:
  • Property in bushfire-prone area - may affect insurance premiums and availability
  • Building in extreme fire risk zones may require BAL-40 or BAL-FZ construction standards
  • Low flood risk favorable for insurance and development approvals
  • Consider multi-peril insurance covering fire, storm, and flood events
  • Climate change implications: increasing heat and fire risk trends evident in 25-year record
